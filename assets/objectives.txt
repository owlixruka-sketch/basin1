IWMI's objectives for the first year of the WEC project is to contribute to Task 2.4: Strengthen GOJ's ability to monitor water resources and improve agricultural land use planning by: Developing water accounting approaches for basin-scale, and in-situ monitoring of water savings. This report details Activity IV: Implement  basin scale water accounting - baseline water status in the selected basin (Amman Zarqa basin).  All scripts, datasets and outputs are available for relevant stakeholders. Further, all scripts and data would be available during the training sessions for all participants.
This report presents the implementation of the developed customized water accounting framework for evaluating and reporting on water budgets for Jordan.  This report builds on the strategic design of a customized water accounting framework and information collected during the water accounting co-design workshop, which included technical representatives from the Ministry of Water and Irrigation (MWI), Ministry of Agriculture (MoA), and stakeholders such as GiZ, FAO, and USAID as well as local experts. 
To this end, the technical design of the customized water accounting framework (WA+) is implemented by a case study application for the Amman Zarqa basin in Jordan.
